## 1. Weather vs. Climate vs. Climatology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Weather** | Short-term atmospheric conditions at a specific time and place |
| **Climate** | Average weather conditions over a long period (typically 30 years) |
| **Climatology** | Scientific study of climates — observed, examined, and understood |
| **Meteorology** | Science dealing with motion and phenomena of the atmosphere; focuses on weather forecasting |

> **Definition**: **Climatology** is the study of atmospheric conditions over a longer period of time, including different kinds of weather at a place and dynamic changes in the atmosphere — treating both long-term and short-term variations.

## 2. Human Contribution to Climate Change

Four primary human activities driving climate change:

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              HUMAN DRIVERS OF CLIMATE CHANGE               │
├────────────────────┬───────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Generating Power  │  Burning fossil fuels → CO₂, SO₂     │
├────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Manufacturing     │  Cement, steel, chemical industry     │
├────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Cutting Forests   │  Deforestation → less CO₂ absorption  │
├────────────────────┼───────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Transportation    │  Vehicles → CO₂, NOₓ, particulates    │
└────────────────────┴───────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Livestock's Additional Contribution**:
- Destruction of forest ecosystems → releases CO₂
- Ruminant digestion of cows/sheep → large amounts of methane (CH₄)
- Meat consumption responsible for releasing CH₄, CO₂, and N₂O

## 3. Greenhouse Gases (GHGs) and Sources

> **Definition**: **Greenhouse gases** are gases in the atmosphere that absorb and re-emit infrared radiation, trapping heat near Earth's surface and causing the greenhouse effect.

| GHG | Source | Potency vs CO₂ |
|-----|--------|----------------|
| **Water Vapour (H₂O)** | Evaporation; most abundant GHG | — (feedback, not forcing) |
| **Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)** | Fossil fuel burning, deforestation, volcanoes | Baseline (1×) |
| **Methane (CH₄)** | Livestock, landfills, rice cultivation, manure | ~25× over 100 years |
| **Nitrous Oxide (N₂O)** | Fertilizers, fossil fuel combustion, biomass burning | ~300× |
| **Sulphur Hexafluoride (SF₆)** | Industrial processes | ~23,000× |
| **HFCs & PFCs** | Industrial refrigerants, electronics | Very high GWP |

> 📌 **Kyoto Protocol GHGs**: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, SF₆, HFCs, PFCs (4 gases + 2 groups)

## 4. Earth Energy Budget

> **Definition**: **Earth's Energy Budget** is the balance between radiant energy received from the Sun (shortwave) and energy emitted back to space (longwave thermal radiation) by the Earth system.

**Energy Flow**:
```
         SUN (shortwave / visible radiation)
                      ↓
        ┌─────────────────────────────┐
        │  ~30% REFLECTED back to     │ ← clouds, aerosols, ice/snow
        │  space (albedo)             │   (Earth's average albedo ~0.3)
        └─────────────────────────────┘
                      ↓
        ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
        │  ~70% ABSORBED by Earth             │
        │  Surface + Atmosphere               │
        └─────────────────────────────────────┘
                      ↓
        ┌────────────────────────────────────────────┐
        │  GREENHOUSE EFFECT:                        │
        │  Outgoing infrared absorbed by H₂O, CO₂,  │
        │  O₃ → re-emitted back downward             │
        │  Net effect: Earth ~33°C warmer than       │
        │  it would be without atmosphere            │
        └────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Key Facts**:
- Sun's energy output varies on an **11-year sunspot cycle** — 'Little Ice Age' in Middle Ages linked to reduced sunspot activity
- Earth is **closer to Sun in January** than June (orbit not perfectly circular) — yet Northern Hemisphere has winter in Jan (axial tilt dominates)
- Without atmosphere, Earth's surface temperature would be **~−18°C** (currently ~+15°C average)

**What affects incoming/outgoing radiation**:

| Factor | Affects | Effect |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Cloud cover | Reflection of solar radiation | More clouds → more reflected → cooler |
| Aerosols/volcanic dust | Scattering and absorption | Cooling (sulfate) or warming (soot) |
| Ice/snow surface | Reflection (high albedo) | Reflects almost all visible radiation |
| Atmospheric GHGs | Absorption of outgoing IR | Traps heat → warming |
| Conduction/convection | Heat loss from surface | Minor pathways |

## 5. Radiative Transfer

> **Definition**: **Radiative transfer** is the physical phenomenon of energy transfer in the form of electromagnetic radiation through a medium, affected by absorption, emission, and scattering processes.

**Four Processes for Incoming Solar Radiation**:
1. **Absorption** — by gases, aerosols, and surface
2. **Reflection** — by clouds, snow, ice, land (albedo)
3. **Scattering** — by atmospheric molecules and particles
4. **Transmission** — directly through the atmosphere to the surface

**Key Principles**:
- Shorter wavelength → **higher energy** carried by that radiation
- Water vapour and CO₂ are **strong absorbers of infrared radiation** but **poor absorbers of visible solar radiation**
- The atmosphere is **transparent** to most visible radiation (passes through)
- Near-infrared (0.75–5 µm) weakly absorbed by H₂O, CO₂, O₃, O₂
- Atmosphere is NOT a perfect blackbody — it **selectively** absorbs and emits at specific wavelengths

**Why this matters**:
- Virtually all Earth–universe energy exchange happens by radiation transfer
- Also major energy exchange between atmosphere and surface, and between atmospheric layers
- This selective absorption is the physical mechanism of the **Greenhouse Effect**

## 6. Naked Planet Climate Model

> **Definition**: The **Naked Planet Climate Model** treats Earth as a body without an atmosphere, applying only Stefan-Boltzmann radiation balance (radiation in = radiation out). It predicts Earth's surface temperature would be approximately **−18°C** without an atmosphere.

**Comparison — Naked Planet vs. Real Earth**:

| Aspect | Naked Planet Model | Real Earth |
|--------|-------------------|------------|
| Atmosphere | None | Full atmosphere with GHGs |
| Greenhouse Effect | Zero | +33°C warming |
| Surface temperature | ~−18°C | ~+15°C (average) |
| Feedbacks | None | Water vapour, ice-albedo, clouds |
| Radiation balance | Shortwave in = longwave out | Includes GHG absorption/re-emission |

**Feedback Mechanisms (Additions to Naked Planet)**:

| Feedback | Type | Mechanism |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Water vapour | Positive | Warming → more evaporation → more H₂O vapour (itself a GHG) → more warming |
| Ice-albedo | Positive | Warming → ice melts → darker ocean → more heat absorbed → more warming |
| Cloud | Mixed | High clouds trap heat (+); Low clouds reflect sunlight (−) |
| Lapse rate | Negative | Upper troposphere warms more than surface → reduces temperature gradient |

## 7. Nuclear Winter

> **Definition**: **Nuclear winter** is a severe global climate disruption predicted to follow a large-scale nuclear war, caused by massive amounts of soot and dust launched into the upper atmosphere — blocking sunlight for months to years.

**Effects**:
- Blockage of sunlight → darkness + radiation + cold → immense disruption to ecosystems
- Drastic reduction in agricultural production → famines and droughts
- Plant and animal life reduced → cascading impacts on human ecosystem
- Affects humans more than any other species (inhaling polluted air, thermal radiation)

**6 Types of Nuclear Winter** (least to most severe):

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Minimal** | Small attack; minimal cloud cover; negligible environmental impact — targeted area only |
| **Marginal** | Larger portion of ash stays in atmosphere; significantly lowers temperatures; impacts agriculture |
| *[Types escalate to full-scale with global crop failure, mass extinction events]* | |

**Nuclear Winter vs. Volcanic Winter**:

| Feature | Nuclear Winter | Volcanic Winter |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Cause | Soot and dust from nuclear fires | Volcanic ash and sulphate aerosols from eruption |
| Duration | Months to years | Months to ~1–2 years |
| Scale | Global (if large war) | Global (if VEI 6+, e.g., Pinatubo 1991) |
| Cooling agent | Soot (absorbs + blocks sunlight) | Sulphate aerosols (reflect sunlight) |
| Similarities | Both block solar radiation; both reduce surface temp and precipitation | |

## 8. Anthropogenic Climate Change — Changes Since Industrial Revolution

> **Definition**: **Anthropogenic climate change** refers to human-caused changes to Earth's climate system, primarily through GHG emissions, deforestation, and land use change — resulting in global warming faster than any other recorded in human history.

**Key Causes**:
- Burning fossil fuels → CO₂ (major driver)
- Aerosol releases → complex regional cooling/warming effects
- Land alteration from agriculture and deforestation
- The Industrial Revolution (beginning ~1750) marks the start of the rapid rise in GHG concentrations

**GHG Concentration Trend**:
- Pre-industrial CO₂: ~**280 ppm**
- 2013: CO₂ surpassed **400 ppm** for first time in human history
- 2024: ~**420 ppm**

**Temperature Rise**:
- Since late 19th century: average surface temperature rose ~**1.1°C (2.0°F)**
- Most warming occurring in the 20th–21st centuries

**Note on Aerosols**: Tropical Pacific has actually *cooled* in some areas due to aerosol release, even as GHGs cause broader warming. This shows complex, non-uniform regional responses.

## 9. Future Trends and Impacts of Climate Change

**Projected Surface Temperature**:
- Projected: ~**1.5°C** by 2050
- Projected: **2–4°C** by 2100
- Even under best-case scenarios, additional warming is locked in

**Precipitation**:
- Global average precipitation increases **~7% for each 1°C** of warming
- With 2°C: heavy rain events become **1.7× more likely** and **14% more intense**
- Higher flood risk in some regions; increased drought in others

**Ocean Acidification (pH)**:
- Ocean surface pH today: ~**8.1** (slightly alkaline)
- Projected by end of 21st century: **8.05–7.75** (depending on CO₂ emissions)
- Cause: oceans absorbing atmospheric CO₂ → forms carbonic acid → lowers pH
- Impact: weakens shells and skeletons of marine organisms (corals, molluscs)
