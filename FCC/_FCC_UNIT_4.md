## 1. International Climate Policy Framework

> **Definition**: The **Climate Change Policy Framework** is the international legal and institutional structure — primarily under the UNFCCC — governing global cooperative efforts to stabilize atmospheric GHG concentrations and address climate change.

**Timeline**:
```
1992 ─── UNFCCC (Earth Summit, Rio de Janeiro) — Framework established
1997 ─── Kyoto Protocol adopted (Kyoto, Japan)
2001 ─── COP7 (Morocco) — LDCF established
2005 ─── Kyoto Protocol entered into force
2009 ─── COP15 (Copenhagen) — $100 billion/year pledge
2015 ─── Paris Agreement adopted (COP21, Paris)
2016 ─── Paris Agreement entered into force
2019 ─── COP25 (Madrid)
2021 ─── COP26 (Glasgow) — Glasgow Climate Pact
2022 ─── COP27 (Sharm El-Sheikh) — Loss & Damage Fund
```

## 2. UNFCCC

> **Definition**: The **United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC)** is an international environmental treaty established in 1992 with the ultimate objective: "stabilization of greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere at a level that would prevent dangerous anthropogenic interference with the climate system."

**Established**: 1992 Earth Summit, Rio de Janeiro

**Party Groups**:

| Group | Countries | Obligations |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **Annex I** | Industrialized countries (OECD 1992 members) + economies in transition (Russia, Baltic States, E. Europe) | Binding emission reduction/limitation targets |
| **Annex II** | Subset of Annex I — wealthy OECD nations | MUST provide financial resources to developing countries |
| **Non-Annex I** | Developing countries | No binding emission limits; general commitments |

**Shared Commitments (All Parties)**:
- Development, application, and diffusion of **climate-friendly technologies**
- Research and **systematic observation** of the climate system
- Education, training, and **public awareness** of climate change
- Improvement of methodologies for **GHG inventories**

**COP**: The **Conference of the Parties** is the supreme decision-making body of the Convention.

## 3. Kyoto Protocol

> **Definition**: The **Kyoto Protocol** is a legally binding protocol to the UNFCCC that commits Annex I (industrialized) countries to quantified greenhouse gas emission reduction targets.

**Key Details**:

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Adopted | **11 December 1997**, Kyoto, Japan |
| Entered into force | **16 February 2005** |
| States ratified | **191** (as of 2011) |
| US position | Signed but **did NOT ratify** |
| Commitment period | **2008–2012** |

**Targets**:
- 37 Annex I countries committed to reduce **4 GHGs + 2 gas groups**: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, SF₆ + HFCs + PFCs
- Collective target: **5.2% reduction** below base year (1990)
- Without US ratification: effective target drops to **4.2%**

**Annex I Obligations**:
- Undertake domestic policies and measures to reduce GHG emissions
- Strive to minimize adverse impacts of these policies on other Parties
- Provide additional financial resources to developing country Parties

## 4. The 3 Kyoto Mechanisms

> These **flexibility mechanisms** allow Annex I countries to meet their binding targets by investing in emission reductions elsewhere.

| Mechanism | Where | Who → Who | Unit Earned |
|-----------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **1. Emissions Trading (ET)** | International market | Annex I ↔ Annex I | AAUs (Assigned Amount Units) |
| **2. Joint Implementation (JI)** | Project in another Annex I country | Annex I → Annex I | ERUs (Emission Reduction Units) |
| **3. Clean Development Mechanism (CDM)** | Project in developing (non-Annex I) country | Annex I → non-Annex I | CERs (Certified Emission Reductions) |

**JI Details**:
- One Annex I Party invests in emission-reducing project in another Annex I country
- Russia accounts for ~**⅔** of JI projected savings (2012); rest split between Ukraine and EU new member states
- Total JI savings ~**one-tenth** that of CDM

**CDM Details**:
- Projects can be emission reduction OR afforestation/reforestation
- CDM creates **NEW** Kyoto units → increases total assigned amount available for Annex I Parties
- Three CER types: **Regular CERs**, **tCERs** (temporary), **lCERs** (long-term)
- India: >**345 registered CDM projects** — most globally (~⅓ of all global projects); China leads in volume (51%), India second (14%)
- Largest Indian CDM project categories: **biomass** and **wind power**

## 5. Carbon Trading — Deep Dive

> **Definition**: **Carbon trading** (Emissions Trading) is a market-based mechanism where a cap is set on total GHG emissions and companies or countries can buy and sell emission allowances within that cap.

**Mechanism**:
```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                 CARBON TRADING SYSTEM                    ║
╠══════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════════╣
║  COUNTRY A           ║  COUNTRY B                        ║
║  (Over-emitter)      ║  (Under-emitter)                  ║
║                      ║                                   ║
║  Allocated:  100 t   ║  Allocated:  100 t                ║
║  Emitted:    120 t   ║  Emitted:     80 t                ║
║  Excess:      20 t   ║  Surplus:     20 t                ║
╠══════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════════╣
║    Country A BUYS 20 credits from Country B              ║
║    Money flows: A → B | Credits flow: B → A              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

**Rules under Kyoto Protocol**:
- Does NOT change the **total assigned amount collectively** — only redistributes
- A Party may acquire **unlimited** units
- Transfer ability limited by **Commitment Period Reserve (CPR)** — minimum units a Party must hold at all times → prevents over-selling

**Carbon Credit vs Carbon Trading**:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Carbon Credit** | A permit allowing emission of **1 tonne CO₂ equivalent** |
| **Carbon Trading** | The buying and selling of carbon credits in a market |
| **Carbon Tax** | A fixed price placed per tonne of CO₂ (no trading, no cap) |

**Types of Carbon Markets**:
1. **Compliance Markets** (mandatory): EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS), Kyoto Protocol ETUs
2. **Voluntary Markets**: Companies voluntarily offset emissions — Gold Standard, VCS

**Purpose and Benefits of Carbon Trading**:
- Makes emission reductions **economically efficient** — cheapest reductions happen first globally
- Creates **financial incentive** for over-achieving reduction targets
- Channels investment to clean development in developing countries (via CDM)
- Allows flexibility: countries with higher abatement costs can pay countries with lower costs

## 6. Paris Agreement (2016)

> **Definition**: The **Paris Agreement** is a legally binding international treaty within the UNFCCC framework on climate change mitigation, adaptation, and finance — adopted at COP21 in December 2015, entered into force November 2016.

**Key Statistics**:
- **196 countries** participated; **189 countries** signed
- Adopted: December 2015 (COP21, Paris)

**Three Core Objectives**:
1. Limit global average temperature increase to **well below 2°C** above pre-industrial; pursue efforts to limit to **1.5°C**
2. Increase ability to **adapt** to adverse impacts; foster climate resilience; not threaten food production
3. Make **finance flows** consistent with low-GHG, climate-resilient development

**20/20/20 Strategy**:
- Reduce CO₂ emissions by **20%**
- Increase renewable energy's market share to **20%**
- **20%** increase in energy efficiency

**Paris vs. Kyoto — Key Differences**:

| Feature | Kyoto Protocol | Paris Agreement |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Nature of commitments | **Legally binding** targets for Annex I | **NDCs** — nationally determined (bottom-up) |
| Coverage | Developed countries only | **Universal** — all 196 parties |
| Structure | Top-down (assigned quantified targets) | Bottom-up (self-declared NDCs) |
| Temperature target | None specified | **1.5°C / well below 2°C** |
| Entered into force | 2005 | 2016 |
| Mechanism | 3 Kyoto Mechanisms | Article 6 carbon markets (still being developed) |

## 7. COP — Conference of Parties

> **Definition**: The **COP (Conference of the Parties)** is the supreme decision-making body of the UNFCCC. All Party states review implementation, adopt decisions, and promote effective execution of the Convention.

**COP Goals**:
- Secure global net zero by mid-century
- Keep warming to **1.5°C**
- Protect communities and natural habitats via adaptation
- Mobilize and deliver climate finance

**Key COPs Summary**:

| COP | Year | Location | Key Outcome |
|-----|------|----------|-------------|
| COP15 | 2009 | Copenhagen | Rich nations pledged **$100 billion/year** to developing nations by 2020 |
| COP16 | 2010 | Cancun | **GCF** established |
| COP21 | 2015 | Paris | **Paris Agreement** adopted |
| COP25 | 2019 | Madrid (orig. Santiago, Chile) | No agreement on Article 6 (carbon markets); no breakthrough on loss & damage |
| COP26 | 2021 | Glasgow, Scotland | **Glasgow Climate Pact**; coal phase-out agreement; $100bn pledge review |
| COP27 | 2022 | Sharm El-Sheikh, Egypt | **Loss and Damage Fund** established (historic for developing nations) |

**COP26 Outcomes (Glasgow)**:
- Glasgow Climate Pact reaffirmed Paris goals
- Many countries revised NDCs upward
- Major agreement to **phase out coal** (timelines and finance details pending)
- Developed countries renewed $100bn commitment (not yet met)

**COP27 Outcomes (Egypt)**:
- **Loss and Damage Fund**: historic agreement; first dedicated fund for countries already experiencing climate disasters
- Reiterated Paris Agreement goals; no significant new emission reduction targets
- Increased focus on adaptation support for developing countries
- Increased youth participation

## 8. India's Role in Climate Change Mitigation

**India's GHG Profile**:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Global GHG rank | **3rd largest** emitter |
| Share of global emissions | ~**5%** |
| Cumulative emissions (since 1850) | Only **2%** of global energy-related |
| Per-capita emissions vs world average | **70% below** |
| Per-capita vs US | **93% below** |
| GHG intensity (GHG/GDP) vs world average | **20% lower** |

**Emission Trend**:
- India's emissions increased **65%** between 1990 and 2005
- Projected to grow another **70%** by 2020 (historical projection)
- Despite GDP growth of ~8%/year, GHG intensity has **declined** (improved efficiency + renewables + public transport + energy pricing reform)

**Energy Mix (historical)**:
- Coal: 39% | Biomass & waste: 29% | Oil: 25% | Natural gas: 5%
- ~500 million people had no access to electricity or modern energy services

**India's International Participation**:
- Party to both **UNFCCC** and **Kyoto Protocol**
- As Non-Annex I: **no binding emission limits** under Kyoto
- Active in **CDM**: >345 projects, most globally; China leads in volume (51%), India 2nd (14%)

**Key National Policies**:
- **National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC)** (2008) — 8 national missions running through 2017
- **Integrated Energy Policy** (2006) — meet energy demand "at least cost, technically efficient, economically viable, and environmentally sustainable"
- Five Year Plans: 11th (2007–12), 12th (Faster, More Inclusive, Sustainable Growth)
- TERI estimated: CO₂ emissions would be ~**20% higher** without current energy policies

**India's Paris Agreement NDCs**:
- Reduce emissions intensity by **45%** by 2030 from 2005 levels
- Achieve **500 GW** non-fossil fuel installed capacity by 2030
- Net zero by **2070**

**India's Climate Performance**:
- **CCPI 2023 rank**: 8th (up from 10th in 2022) — among G20, only India, UK, EU ranked as high performers
- **EPI 2022 rank**: 180th out of 180 (lowest) — low scores on air quality, GHG emissions, rule of law
