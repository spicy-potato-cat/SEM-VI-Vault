## 1. Adaptation — Definition and Types

> **Definition**: **Climate change adaptation** refers to adjustments in natural or human systems in response to actual or expected climatic stimuli and their effects, which moderates harm or exploits beneficial opportunities.

**Types of Adaptation**:

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Anticipatory / Proactive** | Actions taken **before** climate impacts are observed | Moving infrastructure away from floodplains |
| **Reactive** | Actions taken **in response to** observed climate impacts | Restoring mangroves after coastal erosion |
| **Public** | Driven by governments and public institutions | Building sea walls, issuing drought mandates |
| **Private / Planned** | Driven by individuals, businesses | Changing crop types, buying flood insurance |
| **Autonomous** | Spontaneous, without central coordination | Farmers planting earlier due to warming |

> 📌 IPCC distinguishes **anticipatory, reactive, public, and planned** adaptation — but emphasizes all four types are needed, including both **technical measures** (sea walls, crop switching) and **institutional responses** (policy changes, monitoring systems), and **adaptation of natural systems** (assisted migration of species).

## 2. Resilience

> **Definition**: **Resilience** is the capacity of a social, economic, or ecological system to prepare for, withstand, and recover from hazardous climatic events with minimal damage to societal wellbeing, economy, and environment.

**Five Pillars of Resilience**:

| Pillar | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Threshold Capacity** | Ability to absorb shocks without crossing a critical tipping point |
| **Coping Capacity** | Ability to manage and survive ongoing climate stresses |
| **Recovery Capacity** | Ability to bounce back to a stable state after a disturbance |
| **Adaptive Capacity** | Ability to learn and adjust practices over time |
| **Transformative Capacity** | Ability to fundamentally restructure when current systems can no longer function |

**Adaptation vs. Resilience Distinction**:
- **Adaptation** = adjusting systems to live *with* climate change (ongoing process)
- **Resilience** = the *capacity* to cope and recover from climate shocks (a property of the system)

## 3. Adaptation vs. Mitigation

> 📌 **HIGH PRIORITY** — Frequently tested in PYQs, CT-II, and final exam.

> **Definition**: **Mitigation** refers to actions that reduce GHG emissions at the source or enhance carbon sinks — addressing the **cause** of climate change.

> **Definition**: **Adaptation** refers to actions that reduce vulnerability to climate impacts — addressing the **consequences** of climate change.

**Comparison Table**:

| Feature | **Adaptation** | **Mitigation** |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Focus** | Reduce vulnerability to impacts | Reduce GHG emissions / enhance sinks |
| **Addresses** | Consequences of climate change | Cause of climate change |
| **Scale** | Local / regional | Global |
| **Timeframe** | Short to medium term | Long term |
| **Examples** | Sea walls, drought-resistant crops, Miyawaki forests, early warning systems, evacuation plans | Renewable energy, CCS, carbon trading, biofuels, energy efficiency |
| **Cost** | Generally lower per event | High upfront investment, long-term savings |
| **Relationship** | Complementary — not alternatives | Complementary — not alternatives |
| **Key principle** | "Living with change" | "Reducing the change" |

**Critical Point**: Adaptation and mitigation are **both necessary** and **complementary**. Mitigation alone cannot prevent all impacts (warming already committed); adaptation alone cannot solve root causes. The world needs BOTH.

**India CT-II Example** (Set 1):
> Q: "A coastal city builds sea walls to protect against rising sea levels. Is this adaptation or mitigation?"  
> A: **Adaptation** — it reduces vulnerability to the consequence (rising seas) but does not reduce GHG emissions (the cause).

## 4. Climate Change Vulnerability Assessment (CCVA)

> **Definition**: **CCVA (Climate Change Vulnerability Assessment)** is an emerging planning tool used to assess a system's vulnerability to climate change impacts as the first step in designing adaptation strategies.

**Three Core Components**:

| Component | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Exposure** | The nature and degree to which a system is exposed to climate change stresses (e.g., flood zone, drought region) |
| **Sensitivity** | The degree to which a system is affected by climate change exposure (e.g., rain-fed agriculture vs. irrigated) |
| **Adaptive Capacity** | The ability of a system to adjust to climate change impacts, take advantage of opportunities, or cope with consequences |

> 📌 No single standard CCVA framework exists — varies by region, sector, and purpose.

## 5. Climate Finance

> **Definition**: **Climate Finance** refers to local, national, or transnational financing that supports mitigation and adaptation actions that address climate change, sourced from public, private, and alternative sources.

**Origins**:
- Principle of climate finance first set out at **Rio 1992** (developed countries should support developing countries)
- $100 billion/year pledge made at **COP15 Copenhagen 2009**

**Sources and Channels**:

| Source | Channel |
|--------|---------|
| Governments | Direct bilateral aid, contributions to international funds |
| Multilateral Development Banks (MDBs) | Concessional loans, grants |
| International Funds (GCF, GEF) | Grants, equity, guarantees |
| Private Sector | UNEP Finance Initiative; green bonds, insurance, equity |

**Key Principles**:
- Redistribution from developed to developing nations
- Supports both **mitigation** and **adaptation**
- Aims to mobilize private finance at scale through risk reduction instruments

## 6. LDCF — Least Developed Countries Fund

> **Definition**: The **Least Developed Countries Fund (LDCF)** was established at COP7 (Marrakech, Morocco, 2001) to support LDCs in preparing and implementing National Adaptation Programs of Action (NAPAs) — their priority adaptation needs.

**Distribution of LDCF Projects by Sector**:

| Sector | % Share |
|--------|---------|
| Agriculture / Food Security | 29% |
| Disaster Preparedness | 26% |
| Water Resource Management | 19% |
| Community-Level Adaptation | 15% |
| Natural Resource Management | 8% |
| Infrastructure | 2% |
| Health | 1% |

- **46 countries** completed NAPAs (as of last data)
- Governed by GEF (Global Environment Facility), accountable to UNFCCC COP

## 7. Green Climate Fund (GCF)

> **Definition**: The **Green Climate Fund (GCF)** is a major global fund established under the UNFCCC framework to support developing countries in limiting GHG emissions and adapting to climate change.

- Established: **COP16 Cancun** (Decision 1/CP.16)
- Governed by GCF Board, accountable to COP
- Goal: redistribute money from developed → developing world

**Financial Instruments**:

| Instrument | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Grants** | Non-repayable funds — primarily for adaptation in LDCs |
| **Concessional Debt** | Low-interest loans for mitigation/adaptation projects |
| **Guarantees** | Risk insurance to mobilize private sector co-investment |
| **Equity** | Direct equity investment in mitigation opportunities |

**Focus Areas**: Low-emission development, climate-resilient development, ecosystem integrity, human security

---

## 8. PPCR — Pilot Program for Climate Resilience

> **Definition**: The **PPCR (Pilot Program for Climate Resilience)** is a targeted program under the **Strategic Climate Fund (SCF)** that supports highly vulnerable developing countries to integrate climate resilience into core development planning.

**Key Features**:
- Targets developing countries and regions **highly vulnerable** to climate impacts
- Integrates climate resilience into **strategic development planning** (not separate projects)
- Complementary to existing climate funding

> 📌 In highly vulnerable nations (2010–2020), the likelihood of dying in an extreme weather event was **1,500% greater** than in Europe.

## 9. World Bank Group 2025 Climate Change Action Plan

- Published: **22 June 2021**
- Covers: **2021–2025**
- Supports both **mitigation** and **adaptation**

**Key Priorities**:
- Emphasizes **natural capital**, **biodiversity**, and **ecosystem services**
- Increases support for **nature-based solutions** (NbS) for climate resilience
- Aligns financial flows with Paris Agreement goals
- Expands private sector finance mobilization

## 11. Economic Development and Climate Change

**Global Energy Mix** (historical baseline): ~**70%** fossil fuels

**How Temperature Affects Economic Output**:
- **Agricultural yields**: crop stress in heat waves; drought
- **Worker performance**: outdoor workers lose productivity in extreme heat
- **Energy demand**: higher cooling costs; rising energy prices
- **Crime and social unrest**: correlates with temperature extremes

**India's Economic Risk from Climate Change** (projections):
- At **2°C** warming: India GDP reduction of ~**2.6%** by 2100
- At **4°C** warming: India GDP reduction of ~**13.4%** by 2100

**Sectors Most at Risk**:
- Agriculture (reduced yields, food prices)
- Infrastructure (flooding, coastal damage)
- Health and productivity (heat, vector-borne diseases)
- Tourism (coral bleaching, extreme weather)

## 12. Policy Approaches to Adaptation

**Three Main Policy Instruments**:

| Instrument | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Carbon Pricing** | Tax per tonne of CO₂ or cap-and-trade system → incentivizes emission reductions |
| **Technology Subsidies** | Government subsidies for renewable energy, efficiency tech → accelerates transition |
| **Performance Standards** | Regulations mandating minimum efficiency or maximum emission rates (e.g., fuel economy standards) |

**Key Indian Legislation**:

| Act | Year | Focus |
|-----|------|-------|
| **Environment Protection Act** | 1986 | Framework legislation; authority to regulate environmental standards |
| **Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act** | 1974 | Prevention and control of water pollution |
| **National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC)** | 2008 | 8 national missions; India's adaptation and mitigation framework |
