# Personalpronomen – Personal Pronouns

> **Level:** A1 | **Netzwerk Neu A1 Reference:** Kursbuch Kapitel 1–4, Arbeitsbuch Kapitel 1–4 (p. 9, 52, 63–65, 91)

---

## Overview

Personal pronouns are words that replace nouns to avoid repetition. In German, pronouns change form depending on:
1. **Person** — who is speaking / being spoken to / spoken about
2. **Case** — whether the pronoun is the subject (Nominative), direct object (Accusative), or indirect object (Dative)
3. **Gender** — for 3rd person singular, the pronoun must match the **grammatical gender** of the noun it replaces (not biological gender)

> At A1, you need **Nominative** and **Accusative** actively. Dative is introduced later but included here for completeness.

---

## All Personal Pronouns by Case

| Person | Nominativ (Subject) | Akkusativ (Direct Object) | Dativ (Indirect Object) |
|--------|-------------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| 1st sg. | **ich** | **mich** | **mir** |
| 2nd sg. (informal) | **du** | **dich** | **dir** |
| 3rd sg. masculine | **er** | **ihn** | **ihm** |
| 3rd sg. feminine | **sie** | **sie** | **ihr** |
| 3rd sg. neuter | **es** | **es** | **ihm** |
| 1st pl. | **wir** | **uns** | **uns** |
| 2nd pl. (informal) | **ihr** | **euch** | **euch** |
| 3rd pl. | **sie** | **sie** | **ihnen** |
| Formal (sg. + pl.) | **Sie** | **Sie** | **Ihnen** |

---

## Nominative Pronouns — The Subject

Used when the pronoun is **performing** the action:

| Pronoun | English | Example | Translation |
|---------|---------|---------|-------------|
| **ich** | I | Ich komme aus Berlin. | I come from Berlin. |
| **du** | you (sg. informal) | Woher kommst **du**? | Where do you come from? |
| **er** | he / it (m) | **Er** heißt Thomas. | His name is Thomas. |
| **sie** | she / it (f) | **Sie** wohnt in München. | She lives in Munich. |
| **es** | it (n) | **Es** ist ein Hotel. | It is a hotel. |
| **wir** | we | **Wir** kommen aus Spanien. | We come from Spain. |
| **ihr** | you (pl. informal) | Wo wohnt **ihr**? | Where do you (all) live? |
| **sie** | they | **Sie** kommen aus Polen. | They come from Poland. |
| **Sie** | you (formal) | Wie heißen **Sie**? | What is your name? |

> **sie vs. Sie:** Always capitalise **Sie** (formal you). Context and capitalisation distinguish *sie* (she/they) from *Sie* (formal you).

---

## Accusative Pronouns — The Direct Object

Used when the pronoun is **receiving** the action:

| Pronoun | English | Example | Translation |
|---------|---------|---------|-------------|
| **mich** | me | Er kennt **mich**. | He knows me. |
| **dich** | you | Ich sehe **dich**. | I see you. |
| **ihn** | him / it (m) | Ich kaufe **ihn**. | I'm buying it. (m noun) |
| **sie** | her / it (f) | Ich höre **sie**. | I hear her. (f noun) |
| **es** | it (n) | Ich finde **es** schön. | I find it beautiful. |
| **uns** | us | Sie sieht **uns**. | She sees us. |
| **euch** | you (pl.) | Ich mag **euch**. | I like you all. |
| **sie** | them | Ich kenne **sie**. | I know them. |
| **Sie** | you (formal) | Ich verstehe **Sie**. | I understand you. |

---

## Dative Pronouns — The Indirect Object *(A1 preview)*

Used with dative verbs and prepositions (fully covered at A2, but useful to recognise):

| Pronoun | English | Example | Translation |
|---------|---------|---------|-------------|
| **mir** | (to) me | Das gehört **mir**. | That belongs to me. |
| **dir** | (to) you | Ich helfe **dir**. | I'll help you. |
| **ihm** | (to) him/it (m/n) | Ich gebe **ihm** das Buch. | I give him the book. |
| **ihr** | (to) her/it (f) | Ich schreibe **ihr**. | I'm writing to her. |
| **ihm** | (to) it (n) | — | same as masculine |
| **uns** | (to) us | Er hilft **uns**. | He helps us. |
| **euch** | (to) you (pl.) | Ich erkläre **euch**. | I explain to you all. |
| **ihnen** | (to) them | Ich sage **ihnen** Bescheid. | I'll let them know. |
| **Ihnen** | (to) you (formal) | Wie geht es **Ihnen**? | How are you? (formal) |

## How Pronoun Gender Works

The 3rd person pronoun must match the **grammatical gender** of the noun it replaces — not biological sex:

| Noun | Gender | Pronoun (Nom.) | Example |
|------|--------|----------------|---------|
| der Mann | maskulin | **er** | Der Mann kommt. → **Er** kommt. |
| die Frau | feminin | **sie** | Die Frau arbeitet. → **Sie** arbeitet. |
| das Kind | neutrum | **es** | Das Kind schläft. → **Es** schläft. |
| das Mädchen | neutrum (-chen!) | **es** | Das Mädchen heißt Anna. → **Es** heißt Anna. |
| der Bahnhof | maskulin | **er** | Der Bahnhof ist groß. → **Er** ist groß. |
| die Kirche | feminin | **sie** | Die Kirche ist alt. → **Sie** ist alt. |
| das Hotel | neutrum | **es** | Das Hotel ist neu. → **Es** ist neu. |

## Informal vs. Formal Address

| Situation | Pronoun | Verb form | Example |
|-----------|---------|-----------|---------|
| Friend, family, peer, child | **du** | du-form | Wie heißt **du**? |
| Multiple friends / peers | **ihr** | ihr-form | Woher kommt **ihr**? |
| Stranger, adult, authority, professional | **Sie** | sie/Sie-form | Wie heißen **Sie**? |

> **Sie** (formal) always uses the **3rd person plural** verb form, but refers to one or more people.

```
Formal:   Wie heißen Sie?         → What is your name?
Informal: Wie heißt du?           → What's your name?
Informal pl.: Wie heißt ihr?      → What are your names? (to a group)
```

## Common Pitfalls for English Speakers

1. **ihr vs. Ihr vs. ihr (possessive):**
   - **ihr** = you (pl. informal, Nominative)
   - **Ihr** = your (formal possessive) / formal *you* in some contexts
   - **ihr** = her (possessive) / she (Nominative)
   Context is everything.

2. **sie = she, they, or formal You (Sie)** — three meanings, distinguished by capitalisation and verb form:
   ```
   sie kommt    → she comes      (3rd sg. f)
   sie kommen   → they come      (3rd pl.)
   Sie kommen   → you come       (formal)
   ```

3. **das Mädchen → es**, not *sie* — grammatical gender overrides natural gender for pronouns too.

4. **du vs. Sie** — using *du* with a stranger/professor/boss is rude in German. When in doubt, use *Sie*.

5. **Accusative pronoun: er → ihn** — the one masculine pronoun that students forget to change:
   ```
   Er kauft ihn.    ✓   (He buys it — masculine noun)
   Er kauft er.     ✗
   ```

6. **Dative: mir / dir** — don't confuse with Accusative *mich / dich*:
   ```
   Er sieht mich.   → He sees me.        (Akkusativ)
   Er hilft mir.    → He helps me.       (Dativ)
   ```

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1 — Fill in the correct Nominative pronoun

Replace the subject with the correct pronoun:

1. Der Lehrer kommt aus Berlin. → _____ kommt aus Berlin.
2. Die Studentin heißt Maria. → _____ heißt Maria.
3. Das Hotel ist groß. → _____ ist groß.
4. Das Mädchen wohnt in München. → _____ wohnt in München.
5. Mein Bruder und ich spielen Fußball. → _____ spielen Fußball.
6. Du und deine Freunde kommen später. → _____ kommt später.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Er** kommt aus Berlin. (der Lehrer = m)
2. **Sie** heißt Maria. (die Studentin = f)
3. **Es** ist groß. (das Hotel = n)
4. **Es** wohnt in München. (das Mädchen = n, -chen rule!)
5. **Wir** spielen Fußball. (Bruder + ich = wir)
6. **Ihr** kommt später. (du + Freunde = ihr)

</details>

### Exercise 2 — Nominative or Accusative pronoun?

Fill in *er/ihn*, *sie/sie*, *es/es*, *ich/mich*, *du/dich*:

1. _____ kaufe den Anzug. (I am buying…)
2. Siehst du _____? (Can you see me?)
3. Der Mann ist nett. Ich kenne _____. (I know him.)
4. Die Frau heißt Anna. _____ kommt aus Wien. (She comes from Vienna.)
5. Das ist ein Kleid. Ich kaufe _____. (I'm buying it — neuter noun)
6. _____ liebst du? – Ich liebe _____! (Who do you love? – I love you!)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Ich** kaufe den Anzug. (Nom — subject)
2. Siehst du **mich**? (Akk — direct object of *du*)
3. Ich kenne **ihn**. (Akk — der Mann → ihn)
4. **Sie** kommt aus Wien. (Nom — die Frau → sie)
5. Ich kaufe **es**. (Akk — das Kleid → es)
6. **Wen** liebst du? – Ich liebe **dich**! (Akk — direct object)

</details>

### Exercise 3 — du or Sie?

Choose the appropriate form of address and write the question:

1. You meet your professor for the first time. Ask their name.
2. You ask your classmate where they live.
3. You ask a group of friends where they are from.
4. You are at a hotel reception and ask the receptionist for help.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Wie heißen Sie?** (formal — professor)
2. **Wo wohnst du?** (informal — classmate)
3. **Woher kommt ihr?** (informal plural)
4. **Können Sie mir helfen?** / **Wie kann ich Ihnen helfen?** (formal — receptionist)
